Summary

After narrowly escaping death at the hands of North Korean communists, United States soldier Sergeant Zack is saved by Short Round, a South Korean war orphan. Zack, Short Round, and a passing platoon of American soldiers then travel to an abandoned Buddhist temple in search of safety. With its grim and biting realism, its unflattering depictions of American warfare, and its pattern of unsatisfying, realistic conclusions littered throughout its story, The Steel Helmet is considered a striking satire of Hollywood war movies.
